Today, we're going to put some of these ideas together to build our very first type of Arabic sentence! We'll learn about **Basic Nominal Sentences**, which are like simple "X is Y" statements.

## Lesson

1.  **Meet the Nominal Sentence (الْجُمْلَةُ الاسْمِيَّةُ)**
    *   In English, we say "The book *is* new" or "The car *is* fast". We use the word "is".
    *   In Arabic, for simple sentences like "X is Y", we often **don't use a word for "is"**. It's just understood!
    *   These sentences are called **Nominal Sentences (الْجُمْلَةُ الاسْمِيَّةُ)** because they start with a noun (or something that acts like one).

2.  **Parts of a Nominal Sentence**
    *   A basic Nominal Sentence has two main parts:
        *   The **Subject (المُبْتَدَأُ - Al-Mubtada'u)**: This is the first part, the "X" of "X is Y". It's what the sentence is about.
        *   The **Predicate (الْخَبَرُ - Al-Khabaru)**: This is the second part, the "Y" of "X is Y". It tells us something about the subject.

3.  **The Golden Rules for Subject and Predicate**
    *   **The Subject (المُبْتَدَأُ)**:
        *   It always comes first in the sentence.
        *   It is always **definite**. This means it must have `الْـ` (the definite article) at the beginning.
    *   **The Predicate (الْخَبَرُ)**:
        *   It always comes after the subject.
        *   It is always **indefinite**. This means it must have `التَّنْوِينُ` (Tanween Damma, ٌ, for our examples today) at the end.
    *   **Gender Agreement:** If the subject is feminine (ends with تَاء مَرْبُوطَة), then the predicate (if it's a noun describing it) should also be feminine. If the subject is masculine, the predicate should be masculine.

    Think of it like this: `الْـ` + Subject + Predicate + `التَّنْوِينُ` = "The Subject is a Predicate."

## Examples

Let's build some simple Nominal Sentences using words you've learned! Remember, the "is" is implied.

1.  **The pen is a book.**
    *   Our subject is "the pen": `الْقَلَمُ` (al-qalamu)
        *   *Notice*: It has `الْـ` and it's masculine.
    *   Our predicate is "a book": `كِتَابٌ` (kitaabun)
        *   *Notice*: It has `التَّنْوِينُ` and it's masculine, matching the subject.
    *   **Sentence:** `الْقَلَمُ كِتَابٌ.` (al-qalamu kitaabun)

2.  **The school is a library.**
    *   Our subject is "the school": `الْمَدْرَسَةُ` (al-madrasatu)
        *   *Notice*: It has `الْـ` and it's feminine (ends with تَاء مَرْبُوطَة).
    *   Our predicate is "a library": `مَكْتَبَةٌ` (maktabatun)
        *   *Notice*: It has `التَّنْوِينُ` and it's feminine, matching the subject.
    *   **Sentence:** `الْمَدْرَسَةُ مَكْتَبَةٌ.` (al-madrasatu maktabatun)

3.  **The door is an office/desk.**
    *   Our subject is "the door": `الْبَابُ` (al-baabu)
        *   *Notice*: It has `الْـ` and it's masculine.
    *   Our predicate is "an office/desk": `مَكْتَبٌ` (maktabun)
        *   *Notice*: It has `التَّنْوِينُ` and it's masculine, matching the subject.
    *   **Sentence:** `الْبَابُ مَكْتَبٌ.` (al-baabu maktabun)

4.  **The car is a table.**
    *   Our subject is "the car": `السَّيَّارَةُ` (as-sayyaaratu)
        *   *Notice*: It has `الْـ` (and it's a Sun Letter, so the 'L' is silent and the سَ is doubled) and it's feminine.
    *   Our predicate is "a table": `طَاوِلَةٌ` (taawilatun)
        *   *Notice*: It has `التَّنْوِينُ` and it's feminine, matching the subject.
    *   **Sentence:** `السَّيَّارَةُ طَاوِلَةٌ.` (as-sayyaaratu taawilatun)

Great job! You've just learned how to form basic sentences in Arabic! This is a huge step!

Focus on definite/indefinite.)*
