Today, we're going to learn some very useful little words called **Prepositions**. These words help us show where things are or where they are going, like "in," "on," "from," and "to" in English. They are essential for building more complete sentences!

## Lesson

1.  **What are Prepositions?**
    *   Prepositions are words that connect a noun or pronoun to another word in a sentence. They often show location, direction, or time.
    *   Think of "The book is **on** the table," or "I am going **to** school." "On" and "to" are prepositions.
    *   In Arabic, prepositions are small, fixed words that come before a noun.

2.  **Meeting Our First Prepositions**

    Let's learn four important prepositions today:

    *   **فِي (fī)**: This means **"in"** or **"inside"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: It's a long 'ee' sound, like 'fee'.

    *   **عَلَى (ʿalā)**: This means **"on"** or **"upon"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: The 'a' sounds are short, and the last Alif is a long 'aa' sound.

    *   **مِنْ (min)**: This means **"from"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Short 'i' sound, like 'min'.

    *   **إِلَى (ilā)**: This means **"to"** or **"towards"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Short 'i' sound, and the last Alif is a long 'aa' sound, like 'ilaa'.

3.  **The "Kasra Rule" for Nouns After Prepositions**
    *   This is a very important rule for prepositions in Arabic!
    *   When a noun comes directly **after a preposition**, its final vowel sound changes. It will almost always end with a **Kasra ( ِ )** or a **Tanween Kasra ( ٍ )**.
    *   Think of it like the preposition "pulling down" the last vowel sound to a Kasra.
    *   **Examples:**
        *   `مَدْرَسَةٌ` (a school) becomes `مَدْرَسَةٍ` (in **a** school, from **a** school, to **a** school).
        *   `الْمَدْرَسَةُ` (the school) becomes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (in **the** school, from **the** school, to **the** school).
    *   This is how Arabic grammatically links the noun to the preposition.

## Examples

Let's use our new prepositions with words and sentences you already know. Pay close attention to the last vowel sound of the noun that comes after the preposition!

**1. Using فِي (fī) - "in":**

*   **In the school.** (Remember: `الْمَدْرَسَةُ` becomes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` after فِي)
    *   `فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (fī al-madrasati)
*   **In a room.** (Remember: `غُرْفَةٌ` becomes `غُرْفَةٍ` after فِي)
    *   `فِي غُرْفَةٍ.` (fī ghurfatin)
*   **The book is in the car.**
    *   `الْكِتَابُ فِي السَّيَّارَةِ.` (al-kitaabu fī as-sayyaarati.)
        *   *Notice*: `الْكِتَابُ` (The book - Subject), `فِي` (in), `السَّيَّارَةِ` (The car, ending with Kasra).

**2. Using عَلَى (ʿalā) - "on":**

*   **On the table.** (Remember: `الطَّاوِلَةُ` becomes `الطَّاوِلَةِ` after عَلَى)
    *   `عَلَى الطَّاوِلَةِ.` (ʿalā at-taawilati)
*   **On a desk.** (Remember: `مَكْتَبٌ` becomes `مَكْتَبٍ` after عَلَى)
    *   `عَلَى مَكْتَبٍ.` (ʿalā maktabin)
*   **The pen is on the book.**
    *   `الْقَلَمُ عَلَى الْكِتَابِ.` (al-qalamu ʿalā al-kitaabi.)
        *   *Notice*: `الْقَلَمُ` (The pen - Subject), `عَلَى` (on), `الْكِتَابِ` (The book, ending with Kasra).

**3. Using مِنْ (min) - "from":**

*   **From the library.**
    *   `مِنَ الْمَكْتَبَةِ.` (mina al-maktabati)
        *   *Note*: Sometimes the `ن` of مِنْ gets a Fatha for smoother pronunciation when followed by a word starting with `الْـ`. Don't worry too much about this small change, just know it means "from the". `الْمَكْتَبَةِ` (The library, ending with Kasra).
*   **From a teacher.** (masculine)
    *   `مِنْ مُدَرِّسٍ.` (min mudarrisin)
        *   *Notice*: `مُدَرِّسٌ` becomes `مُدَرِّسٍ` (a teacher, ending with Tanween Kasra).

**4. Using إِلَى (ilā) - "to":**

*   **To the university.**
    *   `إِلَى الْجَامِعَةِ.` (ilā al-jaamiʿati)
        *   *Notice*: `الْجَامِعَةِ` (The university, ending with Kasra).
*   **To a car.**
    *   `إِلَى سَيَّارَةٍ.` (ilā sayyaartin)
        *   *Notice*: `سَيَّارَةٌ` becomes `سَيَّارَةٍ` (a car, ending with Tanween Kasra).
*   **He is to the school.** (Meaning: He is going to school/He is at the school's location.)
    *   `هُوَ إِلَى الْمَدْرَسَةِ.` (huwa ilā al-madrasati)
        *   *Notice*: `هُوَ` (He - Subject), `إِلَى` (to), `الْمَدْرَسَةِ` (The school, ending with Kasra).

You've just learned how to use prepositions to make your Arabic sentences much more descriptive! This is a big step in expressing location and movement.
