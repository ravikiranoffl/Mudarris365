Today, we're going to learn about words that point things out: **Demonstrative Pronouns**. These are like saying "this" or "that" in English. Just like nouns, these words also have masculine and feminine forms in Arabic!

## Lesson

1.  **What are Demonstrative Pronouns?**
    *   Demonstrative pronouns are words we use to point to specific things. In English, we use "this" for something close to us, and "that" for something further away.
    *   In Arabic, these words also change depending on whether the thing you're pointing to is masculine or feminine. For now, we'll focus on singular items (one thing).

2.  **"This" in Arabic (for things close by)**

    *   **هَذَا (Hādhā)**: This means **"This"** and is used for **masculine** nouns (things that don't end in تَاء مَرْبُوطَة).
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: The initial Alif is just a seat for the Hamza; the long 'aa' sound comes from the Alif after the Dhāl ( ذ ).
    *   **هَذِهِ (Hādhihi)**: This means **"This"** and is used for **feminine** nouns (things that end in تَاء مَرْبُوطَة).
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Both the 'a' and 'i' are short vowel sounds.

3.  **"That" in Arabic (for things far away)**

    *   **ذَلِكَ (Dhālika)**: This means **"That"** and is used for **masculine** nouns.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: The long 'aa' sound comes from the Alif after the Dhāl ( ذ ).
    *   **تِلْكَ (Tilka)**: This means **"That"** and is used for **feminine** nouns.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Both the 'i' and 'a' are short vowel sounds.

4.  **Using Demonstrative Pronouns in Sentences**
    *   When you use these words to say "This *is* a book" or "That *is* a car," you're forming a basic Nominal Sentence, just like we learned on Day 6!
    *   The demonstrative pronoun (هَذَا, هَذِهِ, ذَلِكَ, تِلْكَ) acts as the **Subject (المُبْتَدَأُ)**. It is naturally definite.
    *   The noun that follows it acts as the **Predicate (الْخَبَرُ)**. It tells us what "this" or "that" is, and it must be **indefinite** (ending with Tanween Damma ٌ).
    *   Remember: The demonstrative pronoun must match the gender of the noun it's referring to!

    So, the structure will be: **Demonstrative Pronoun (definite) + Noun (indefinite with Tanween Damma ٌ)**. The "is" is understood!

## Examples

Let's use our new demonstrative pronouns with some nouns you already know! Pay close attention to the gender match and the Tanween on the noun.

**1. "This" (for close things):**

*   **This is a book.** (`كِتَابٌ` is masculine)
    *   `هَذَا كِتَابٌ.` (Hādhā kitaabun.)
        *   *Notice*: `هَذَا` is masculine. `كِتَابٌ` is masculine and indefinite.
*   **This is a school.** (`مَدْرَسَةٌ` is feminine, ends with تَاء مَرْبُوطَة)
    *   `هَذِهِ مَدْرَسَةٌ.` (Hādhihi madrasatun.)
        *   *Notice*: `هَذِهِ` is feminine. `مَدْرَسَةٌ` is feminine and indefinite.
*   **This is a teacher.** (`مُدَرِّسٌ` is masculine)
    *   `هَذَا مُدَرِّسٌ.` (Hādhā mudarrisun.)
*   **This is a student.** (`طَالِبَةٌ` is feminine)
    *   `هَذِهِ طَالِبَةٌ.` (Hādhihi taalibatun.)

**2. "That" (for far away things):**

*   **That is a pen.** (`قَلَمٌ` is masculine)
    *   `ذَلِكَ قَلَمٌ.` (Dhālika qalamun.)
        *   *Notice*: `ذَلِكَ` is masculine. `قَلَمٌ` is masculine and indefinite.
*   **That is a car.** (`سَيَّارَةٌ` is feminine)
    *   `تِلْكَ سَيَّارَةٌ.` (Tilka sayyaaratun.)
        *   *Notice*: `تِلْكَ` is feminine. `سَيَّارَةٌ` is feminine and indefinite.
*   **That is a door.** (`بَابٌ` is masculine)
    *   `ذَلِكَ بَابٌ.` (Dhālika baabun.)
*   **That is a library.** (`مَكْتَبَةٌ` is feminine)
    *   `تِلْكَ مَكْتَبَةٌ.` (Tilka maktabatun.)

You can now point to things and identify them in Arabic! This is a fantastic step in building your communication skills.
