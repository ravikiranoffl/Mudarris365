Today, we're going to learn how to ask questions! We'll introduce four important **Question Words**: "Who," "What," "Where," and "When." These words are like keys that unlock information!

## Lesson

1.  **What are Question Words?**
    *   Question words are special words we use at the beginning of a sentence to ask for specific information.
    *   In English, we have words like "Who?", "What?", "Where?", "When?", "Why?", "How?".
    *   In Arabic, we also have specific words for these. We'll learn four of them today.

2.  **Meeting Our First Question Words**

    Let's learn these important words one by one:

    *   **مَنْ (man)**: This means **"Who?"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Short 'a' sound, like 'man'. Used for people.

    *   **مَا (mā)**: This means **"What?"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Long 'aa' sound, like 'maa'. Used for things, especially when asking for identification ("What is this?").

    *   **أَيْنَ (ayna)**: This means **"Where?"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: 'ay' as in 'play', short 'na'. Like 'ayna'. Used for location.

    *   **مَتَى (matā)**: This means **"When?"**.
        *   *Pronunciation hint*: Short 'ma', long 'aa' sound, like 'mataa'. Used for time.

3.  **Using Question Words in Sentences**
    *   In Arabic, these question words usually come at the beginning of the sentence.
    *   We can use them with the pronouns, demonstrative pronouns, and nouns you've already learned.
    *   The structure often remains similar to our nominal sentences, with the question word simply replacing the "unknown" part.

## Examples

Let's use our new question words to form some questions!

**1. Asking "Who?" with مَنْ (man):**

*   **Who is he?**
    *   `مَنْ هُوَ؟` (man huwa?)
        *   *Notice*: `مَنْ` (Who) comes first, followed by the pronoun `هُوَ` (he).
*   **Who is she?**
    *   `مَنْ هِيَ؟` (man hiya?)
*   **Who are you?** (addressed to a man)
    *   `مَنْ أَنْتَ؟` (man anta?)
*   **Who is the teacher?**
    *   `مَنْ الْمُدَرِّسُ؟` (man al-mudarrisu?)
        *   *Notice*: `الْمُدَرِّسُ` is definite (has `الْـ`) as it refers to a specific teacher.

**2. Asking "What?" with مَا (mā):**

*   **What is this?** (referring to a masculine noun like `كِتَابٌ` "book")
    *   `مَا هَذَا؟` (mā hādhā?)
        *   *Notice*: `مَا` (What) comes first, followed by `هَذَا` (this).
*   **What is this?** (referring to a feminine noun like `سَيَّارَةٌ` "car")
    *   `مَا هَذِهِ؟` (mā hādhihi?)
*   **What is that?** (referring to a masculine noun like `قَلَمٌ` "pen")
    *   `مَا ذَلِكَ؟` (mā dhālika?)
*   **What is that?** (referring to a feminine noun like `مَدْرَسَةٌ` "school")
    *   `مَا تِلْكَ؟` (mā tilka?)

**3. Asking "Where?" with أَيْنَ (ayna):**

*   **Where is the book?**
    *   `أَيْنَ الْكِتَابُ؟` (ayna al-kitaabu?)
        *   *Notice*: `أَيْنَ` (Where) comes first, followed by the definite noun `الْكِتَابُ` (the book).
*   **Where is the car?**
    *   `أَيْنَ السَّيَّارَةُ؟` (ayna as-sayyaaratu?)
        *   *Remember Sun Letter rule for `السَّيَّارَةُ`!*
*   **Where is your (masculine) teacher?**
    *   `أَيْنَ مُدَرِّسُكَ؟` (ayna mudarrisuka?)
        *   *Notice*: We use the noun with an attached pronoun (`مُدَرِّسُكَ` - your teacher).
*   **Where is the new school?**
    *   `أَيْنَ الْمَدْرَسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ؟` (ayna al-madrasatu al-jadeedatu?)
        *   *Notice*: We use a definite noun with a definite adjective (`الْمَدْرَسَةُ الْجَدِيدَةُ`).

**4. Asking "When?" with مَتَى (matā):**

*   **When?** (standalone question)
    *   `مَتَى؟` (matā?)
*   **When is the school?** (Meaning "When is the school event/class?")
    *   `مَتَى الْمَدْرَسَةُ؟` (matā al-madrasatu?)
        *   *Notice*: `مَتَى` (When) comes first, followed by the definite noun `الْمَدْرَسَةُ`.
*   **When is the university?** (Meaning "When is the university event/class?")
    *   `مَتَى الْجَامِعَةُ؟` (matā al-jaamiʿatu?)

You can now ask basic questions in Arabic! This is a huge step in having real conversations.
