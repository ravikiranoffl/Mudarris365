## Lesson

1.  **The Universal Greeting: "Peace be upon you"**
    *   The most common way to greet someone in Arabic, at any time of day, is to say:
        *   `السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.` (As-salāmu 'alaykum.)
            *   This literally means "Peace be upon you."
            *   `السَّلَامُ` means "the peace."
            *   `عَلَى` means "on" or "upon" (a preposition you learned!).
            *   `ـكُمْ` is an attached pronoun meaning "you all" or "you (plural)". Even if you're speaking to one person, this plural form is used for respect and as a general greeting.
    *   The proper response to this greeting is:
        *   `وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَامُ.` (Wa 'alaykumus-salām.)
            *   This means "And upon you be peace."
            *   `وَ` means "and."
            *   Notice the `ُ` (Damma) on the `م` of `عَلَيْكُمُ`. This happens for smooth pronunciation when it's followed by a word starting with `الْـ` (like `السَّلَامُ`).

2.  **Time-Specific Greetings: "Good Morning" and "Good Evening"**
    *   Just like in English, you can also give time-specific greetings:
        *   **Good Morning:**
            *   `صَبَاحُ الْخَيْرِ.` (Sabāḥul khayr.)
                *   `صَبَاحُ` means "morning."
                *   `الْخَيْرِ` means "the goodness." So, "Morning of goodness."
                *   Notice the `ِ` (Kasra) at the end of `الْخَيْرِ`. This is a grammatical rule in Arabic for phrases like "morning of goodness." For now, just learn this as a fixed expression.
            *   **Response for Good Morning:**
                *   `صَبَاحُ النُّورِ.` (Sabāḥun-nūr.)
                    *   `النُّورِ` means "the light." So, "Morning of light." This is a common and beautiful response.
        *   **Good Evening:**
            *   `مَسَاءُ الْخَيْرِ.` (Masā'ul khayr.)
                *   `مَسَاءُ` means "evening." So, "Evening of goodness."
            *   **Response for Good Evening:**
                *   `مَسَاءُ النُّورِ.` (Masā'un-nūr.)
                    *   "Evening of light."

3.  **Asking "How are you?"**
    *   Once you've greeted someone, a common next step is to ask how they are doing:
        *   **To a man:** `كَيْفَ حَالُكَ؟` (Kayfa ḥāluka?)
            *   `كَيْفَ` is a new question word meaning **"How?"**
            *   `حَالٌ` is a new noun meaning "condition" or "state."
            *   `ـكَ` is our familiar attached pronoun for "your" (masculine singular).
            *   So, "How your condition?"
        *   **To a woman:** `كَيْفَ حَالُكِ؟` (Kayfa ḥāluki?)
            *   Same as above, but with `ـكِ` for "your" (feminine singular).

    *   **Responding to "How are you?":**
        *   `أَنَا بِخَيْرٍ، شُكْرًا.` (Ana bi-khayrin, shukran.)
            *   `أَنَا` (I) is an independent pronoun you learned.
            *   `بِـ` is a new attached preposition meaning **"with"** or **"by"**. Just like other prepositions, the noun after it takes a Kasra!
            *   `خَيْرٍ` means "goodness" (here, indefinite with Tanween Kasra due to `بِـ`). So, "I am with goodness."
            *   `شُكْرًا` means **"Thanks"** or "Thank you." This is a common fixed expression with Tanween Fatha.

4.  **Asking "What is your name?" and Telling Your Name**
    *   To ask someone's name:
        *   **To a man:** `مَا اسْمُكَ؟` (Mā ismuka?)
            *   `مَا` (What?) is a question word you learned.
            *   `اِسْمٌ` is a new noun meaning **"name."**
            *   `ـكَ` is the attached pronoun "your" (masculine singular). So, "What your name?"
        *   **To a woman:** `مَا اسْمُكِ؟` (Mā ismuki?)
            *   Same, but with `ـكِ` for "your" (feminine singular).
    *   To say your name:
        *   `اِسْمِي...` (Ismī...)
            *   `اِسْمٌ` (name) + `ـِي` (my) = "My name is..."
            *   Then you say your name (e.g., `اِسْمِي مُدَرِّسٌ.` = My name is Mudarris).

5.  **"Nice to meet you!"**
    *   A common phrase to say after introductions is:
        *   `تَشَرَّفْنَا.` (Tasharrafnā.)
            *   This is a fixed expression that means **"We are honored"** or "Pleased to meet you." Don't worry about breaking down the parts of this one for now; just learn it as a whole phrase!

You've just learned how to greet people, ask how they are, introduce yourselves, and say "nice to meet you" in Arabic! This is incredibly exciting!

## Examples

Let's put some of these greetings and introductions into practice. Imagine two people, Ahmed (أَحْمَدُ) and Fatimah (فَاطِمَةُ), are meeting.

**Dialogue 1: Meeting in the morning (Ahmed is speaking to Fatimah)**

*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.` (As-salāmu 'alaykum.)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَامُ.` (Wa 'alaykumus-salām.)
*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `صَبَاحُ الْخَيْرِ.` (Sabāḥul khayr.)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `صَبَاحُ النُّورِ.` (Sabāḥun-nūr.)
*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `كَيْفَ حَالُكِ؟` (Kayfa ḥāluki?)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `أَنَا بِخَيْرٍ، شُكْرًا.` (Ana bi-khayrin, shukran.)

**Dialogue 2: Meeting in the evening (Fatimah is speaking to Ahmed)**

*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ.` (As-salāmu 'alaykum.)
*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `وَعَلَيْكُمُ السَّلَامُ.` (Wa 'alaykumus-salām.)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `مَسَاءُ الْخَيْرِ.` (Masā'ul khayr.)
*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `مَسَاءُ النُّورِ.` (Masā'un-nūr.)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `مَا اسْمُكَ؟` (Mā ismuka?)
*   **أَحْمَدُ:** `اِسْمِي أَحْمَدُ.` (Ismī Aḥmadu.)
*   **فَاطِمَةُ:** `تَشَرَّفْنَا.` (Tasharrafnā.)

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   السَّلَامُ (As-salāmu) - peace
*   عَلَيْكُمْ ('alaykum) - upon you (plural/general)
*   وَعَلَيْكُمُ (Wa 'alaykum) - and upon you (for the response)
*   صَبَاحُ (Sabāḥu) - morning (part of fixed expression)
*   الْخَيْرِ (Al-khayr) - the goodness (part of fixed expression)
*   النُّورِ (An-nūr) - the light (part of fixed expression)
*   مَسَاءُ (Masā'u) - evening (part of fixed expression)
*   كَيْفَ (Kayfa) - how
*   حَالٌ (Ḥālun) - condition, state
*   بِـ (Bi-) - with, by (an attached preposition)
*   شُكْرًا (Shukran) - thanks, thank you
*   اِسْمٌ (Ismun) - name
*   تَشَرَّفْنَا (Tasharrafnā) - We are honored / Pleased to meet you
