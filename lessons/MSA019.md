Today is another huge day! We are going to learn how to talk about actions that are happening **now** or happen **regularly** – this is called the **Present Tense (الْمُضَارِعُ - al-mudhaari'u)**. Specifically, we'll learn how to conjugate verbs in the present tense for "he," "she," and "they."

## Lesson

1.  **What is the Present Tense?**
    *   The Present Tense describes actions that are happening at the moment you are speaking, or actions that happen habitually (every day, usually, etc.).
    *   In English, "He writes" or "He is writing" are both examples of the present tense. Arabic often uses one form for both!

2.  **Prefixes for the Present Tense**
    *   Remember how with past tense verbs, we added *suffixes* (endings) to the "he" form to show who did the action?
    *   With present tense verbs, we primarily use **prefixes** (beginnings) to show who is doing the action. The root letters will still be there in the middle, but a new letter will come *before* them!

3.  **The Present Tense Verb for "He" (هُوَ)**
    *   We use the prefix **يَـ** (ya-) for "he."
    *   **Structure:** `يَـفْعَلُ` (yaf'alu) - This is a common pattern for many "Form I" present tense verbs. It usually has a Fatha ( َ ) on the prefix, a Sukoon ( ْ ) on the first root letter, a Damma ( ُ ) on the second root letter, and a Damma ( ُ ) on the third root letter.
    *   **Example:** Let's take our root `ك ت ب` (meaning "writing").
        *   Add `يَـ` to the beginning: `يَكْتُبُ` (yaktubu) - **He writes / He is writing.**
            *   *Notice*: `يَـ` (prefix), `كْ` (first root letter with Sukoon), `تُ` (second root letter with Damma), `بُ` (third root letter with Damma).
    *   **Another example:** With the root `د ر س` (meaning "study").
        *   `يَدْرُسُ` (yadrusu) - **He studies / He is studying.**

4.  **The Present Tense Verb for "She" (هِيَ)**
    *   We use the prefix **تَـ** (ta-) for "she."
    *   **Structure:** `تَـفْعَلُ` (taf'alu) - The pattern is similar to the "he" form, just with a different prefix.
    *   **Example:**
        *   Take our root `ك ت ب`.
        *   Add `تَـ` to the beginning: `تَكْتُبُ` (taktubu) - **She writes / She is writing.**
            *   *Notice*: `تَـ` (prefix), `كْ` (first root letter with Sukoon), `تُ` (second root letter with Damma), `بُ` (third root letter with Damma).
    *   **Another example:** With the root `د ر س`.
        *   `تَدْرُسُ` (tadrusu) - **She studies / She is studying.**

5.  **The Present Tense Verb for "They" (هُُمْ) (Masculine/Mixed Plural)**
    *   For "they" (masculine or mixed groups), we use the same prefix as "he" (`يَـ`), but we also add a special ending to the verb.
    *   **Structure:** `يَـفْعَلُونَ` (yaf'alūna) - The prefix `يَـ` (ya-), then the root, followed by a long `ـُو` (Waw) and `نَ` (na).
    *   **Example:**
        *   Take our root `ك ت ب`.
        *   Add `يَـ` to the beginning and `ونَ` to the end: `يَكْتُبُونَ` (yaktubūna) - **They write / They are writing.**
            *   *Notice*: `يَـ` (prefix), `كْ` (first root letter with Sukoon), `تُ` (second root letter with Damma), `بُو` (third root letter with long Waw), `نَ` (Nun with Fatha).
    *   **Another example:** With the root `د ر س`.
        *   `يَدْرُسُونَ` (yadrusūna) - **They study / They are studying.**

This is a new way for the verb to show who is doing the action, using prefixes and sometimes suffixes together! The good news is that these patterns are very consistent.

## Examples

Let's put these new present tense verb forms into practice with the pronouns you learned on Day 7, and some nouns you already know!

1.  **He writes.**
    *   `هُوَ يَكْتُبُ.` (Huwa yaktubu.)

2.  **She writes.**
    *   `هِيَ تَكْتُبُ.` (Hiya taktubu.)

3.  **They (masculine) write.**
    *   `هُمْ يَكْتُبُونَ.` (Hum yaktubūna.)

4.  **He studies the book.**
    *   `هُوَ يَدْرُسُ الْكِتَابَ.` (Huwa yadrusu al-kitaaba.)
        *   *Notice*: `الْكِتَابَ` (the book) takes a Fatha because it is the object of the verb.

5.  **She studies in the new school.**
    *   `هِيَ تَدْرُسُ فِي الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ.` (Hiya tadrusu fī al-madrasati al-jadeedati.)
        *   *Notice*: `فِي` (in) still makes `الْمَدْرَسَةِ الْجَدِيدَةِ` end with Kasra, and the adjective matches the noun.

6.  **The student (masculine) writes in his book.**
    *   `الطَّالِبُ يَكْتُبُ فِي كِتَابِهِ.` (At-taalibu yaktubu fī kitaabihi.)
        *   *Notice*: `الطَّالِبُ` (the student - masculine) takes the place of `هُوَ`. `كِتَابِهِ` (his book) uses an attached pronoun and takes a Kasra because of `فِي`.

7.  **The teachers (masculine) study the big book.**
    *   `الْمُدَرِّسُونَ يَدْرُسُونَ الْكِتَابَ الْكَبِيرَ.` (Al-mudarrisūna yadrusūna al-kitaaba al-kabeera.)
        *   *Notice*: `الْمُدَرِّسُونَ` is a plural subject.

You've just learned how to express actions in the present tense for "he," "she," and "they"! This is a significant step in making your Arabic conversations much more dynamic.

## 📚 Core Vocabulary
*   الْمُضَارِعُ (al-mudhaari'u) - the present tense
*   يَكْتُبُ (yaktubu) - he writes / he is writing
*   تَكْتُبُ (taktubu) - she writes / she is writing
*   يَكْتُبُونَ (yaktubūna) - they (masculine/mixed plural) write / are writing
*   يَدْرُسُ (yadrusu) - he studies / he is studying
*   تَدْرُسُ (tadrusu) - she studies / she is studying
*   يَدْرُسُونَ (yadrusūna) - they (masculine/mixed plural) study / are studying
